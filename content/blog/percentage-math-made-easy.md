## Quick Answer

**Quick Answer:** To find **X% of Y**, multiply **Y × (X ÷ 100)**. Example: 20% of $49.99 = $49.99 × 0.20 = **$9.998** (about **$10.00** off). Try our [Percentage Calculator](/tools/calc/percentage-calculator).

## Three common percentage questions

### 1. What is X% of Y?

**Formula:** `(X / 100) × Y`

Shopping example: 25% off a $80 jacket → 0.25 × 80 = **$20 off** → pay **$60**.

### 2. X is what % of Y?

**Formula:** `(X / Y) × 100`

Test score: 42 out of 50 → (42/50) × 100 = **84%**.

### 3. Percent change from A to B

**Formula:** `((B - A) / A) × 100`

Price rose from $80 to $100 → ((100-80)/80) × 100 = **+25%**.

## Percentage tips

- Convert percent to decimal by dividing by 100 (20% → 0.20).
- For mental math, 10% is easy — double it for 20%, halve it for 5%.
- Sales tax and tips are just “percent of” problems.
