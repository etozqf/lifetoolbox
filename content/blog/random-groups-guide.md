## Quick Answer

**Quick Answer:** Paste one name per line, choose **number of groups** or **people per group**, and click shuffle. Our [Random Group Generator](/tools/random/random-groups) splits everyone fairly with a single random draw.

## When to use random groups

- **Classroom activities** — mix students for projects
- **Workshop icebreakers** — balanced discussion tables
- **Sports & games** — pick sides without bias
- **Giveaways** — split entrants into prize pools

## Tips for fair splits

- Remove empty lines before shuffling
- Use **Regenerate** if someone is absent after the first split
- For picking **one** winner, try [Name Picker](/tools/random/name-picker) instead
- For numeric draws, use [Random Number Generator](/tools/random/number-generator)

## Group size vs number of groups

| Mode | Example (12 people) |
|------|---------------------|
| 3 groups | 4 + 4 + 4 (or 4+4+3+1 if uneven—tool balances) |
| 4 per group | 3 groups of 4 |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the shuffle truly random?

We use Fisher-Yates shuffle in the browser—fair for everyday use, not cryptographic security.

### Duplicate names?

Duplicates are treated as separate entries; dedupe your list first if needed.

### Can I export results?

Copy each group from the on-screen results, or screenshot for sharing in chat apps.
