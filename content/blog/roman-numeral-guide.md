## Quick Answer

**Quick Answer:** Roman numerals use letters **I, V, X, L, C, D, M** for 1, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500, 1000. Example: **2024 = MMXXIV**. Convert both ways with our [Roman Numeral Converter](/tools/calc/roman-numeral).

## Basic symbols

| Symbol | Value |
|--------|-------|
| I | 1 |
| V | 5 |
| X | 10 |
| L | 50 |
| C | 100 |
| D | 500 |
| M | 1000 |

## Subtractive notation

- **IV** = 4 (5 − 1), not IIII
- **IX** = 9 (10 − 1)
- **XL** = 40, **XC** = 90, **CD** = 400, **CM** = 900

## Examples

| Arabic | Roman |
|--------|-------|
| 14 | XIV |
| 49 | XLIX |
| 1999 | MCMXCIX |
| 2024 | MMXXIV |

Standard modern form supports **1–3999** (no more than three repeated I, X, or C in a row).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why no zero in Roman numerals?

Ancient Romans had no symbol for zero; the system starts at I (1).

### Clock faces use IIII for 4?

Some clocks use **IIII** instead of **IV** for aesthetics—our tool follows standard subtractive rules.
