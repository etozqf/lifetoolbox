## Quick Answer

**Quick Answer:** In the United States, a standard restaurant tip is **15–20% of the bill after tax**. For a $60 bill with an 18% tip, you leave **$10.80**, for a total of **$70.80**. Use our [Tip Calculator](/tools/calc/tip-calculator) to split bills instantly.

## Tipping standards in the US

Most sit-down restaurants expect **15%** as a minimum and **18–20%** for good service. For large groups, some restaurants add an automatic **18% gratuity** — check your receipt before tipping twice.

## How to calculate tip step by step

1. Take your bill total (usually after tax).
2. Multiply by the tip percentage (e.g. 0.18 for 18%).
3. Add that amount to the bill for the grand total.
4. Divide by the number of people if splitting.

**Example:** $60 × 18% = $10.80 tip → $70.80 total → $35.40 each for 2 people.

## Before tax vs after tax

Many people tip on the **after-tax** total because that matches what they pay. Either approach is common — pick one and stay consistent.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I tip on a $50 bill?

At 18%, tip **$9.00** for a total of **$59.00**.

### Is 20% tip standard in 2026?

In the US, **18–20%** is widely considered standard for sit-down dining.

### Should I tip on takeout?

Takeout tipping is optional but **10%** or a few dollars is appreciated for large or complex orders.
